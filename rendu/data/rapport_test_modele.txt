Q1: What is compound interest?
R : Compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal
    amount and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.
    In other words, interest is earned on top of interest. This results
    in a higher total interest compared to simple interest, where interest
    is only calculated on the principal amount. The compounding can occur
    continuously or at regular intervals, such as monthly or yearly.
    The key feature of compound interest is that the interest calculation
    incorporates past interest when calculating new interest, leading to
    exponential growth over time.

Q2: How should I build a diversified investment portfolio?
R : Building a diversified investment portfolio involves investing in a mix
    of assets that respond differently to economic conditions. This helps
    reduce risk since not all asset classes move in the same direction at
    the same time. Key points: invest in a variety of asset classes
    (stocks, bonds, real estate...), consider geographic diversification,
    rebalance regularly.
    Temps : 24.6s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q3: What is the difference between stocks and bonds?
R : Stocks: buying partial ownership in a company, value grows with company
    profits but carries more risk. Bonds: lending money to a company or
    government in exchange for regular interest payments, lower risk but
    lower returns. Stocks offer higher growth potential, bonds offer more
    stability.
    Temps : 23.0s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q4: How do I create a personal budget?
R : Steps: 1) Calculate total monthly income from all sources. 2) List
    all recurring expenses (rent, food, transport...). 3) Categorize
    expenses as fixed or variable. 4) Set savings goals. 5) Track
    spending and adjust regularly. The 50/30/20 rule is a good starting
    point (50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings).
    Temps : 40.5s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q5: What is inflation and how does it affect my savings?
R : Inflation is a general increase in prices and fall in purchasing power
    of money. It impacts savings by: 1) Reducing purchasing power over
    time. 2) If savings interest rate is below inflation rate, real value
    of savings decreases. 3) Encourages investing in assets that
    outperform inflation (stocks, real estate...).
    Temps : 49.8s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q6: What is a mutual fund?
R : A mutual fund is an investment vehicle made up of a pool of money
    collected from many investors. Managed by professional money managers
    who invest in various securities like stocks and bonds. Achieves
    diversification by spreading investments across many assets.
    Accessible to small investors who couldn't otherwise diversify.
    Temps : 14.2s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q7: How does the stock market work?
R : The stock market allows companies to sell shares of ownership to raise
    capital from investors. After an IPO, shares can be bought and sold
    among investors. Prices are determined by supply and demand. Stock
    exchanges (NYSE, NASDAQ...) provide a regulated marketplace.
    Investors make money through price appreciation and dividends.
    Temps : 8.3s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q8: What are the risks of investing in cryptocurrency?
R : Main risks: Volatility (high price swings, significant losses possible),
    Regulatory risk (unregulated space, laws can change quickly),
    Security risk (hacking, loss of wallet access), Liquidity risk
    (some cryptos hard to sell quickly), Scams and fraud are common
    in the crypto space.
    Temps : 49.3s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q9: How do I save for retirement?
R : Steps: 1) Define financial goals and retirement timeline. 2) Assess
    income and expenses. 3) Start early to benefit from compound interest.
    4) Contribute to retirement accounts (401k, IRA or equivalent).
    5) Invest in a diversified portfolio. 6) Increase contributions
    over time as income grows.
    Temps : 43.0s | Qualite : BONNE

------------------------------------------------------

Q10: What is the difference between gross and net income?
R : Gross income is the total revenue generated before any deductions
    (expenses, taxes). Net income is what remains after subtracting
    all costs and taxes. For individuals: gross = salary before tax,
    net = take-home pay. Net income is the real indicator of
    financial health.
    Temps : 12.3s | Qualite : BONNE
